What Is The CreditCard Collection?

The CreditCard Collection is an independent, educational ecosystem about credit cards and payment systems. It combines a main comparison hub at Choose.Creditcard with multiple topic hubs and dozens of minisites on descriptive .creditcard domains.

Each minisite explains a single concept — such as travel cards, technology, loyalty programs or credit scores — and then links back to neutral comparison and documentation pages on the main hub.

How Hubs and Minisites Fit Together

The structure is designed to be predictable. Most pages fall into one of three roles: hubs, spokes or infrastructure pages.

  • Hubs – overview pages on Choose.Creditcard (for example travel, technology, rewards, protections).
  • Spokes – minisites on .creditcard domains that explain one idea in depth and link back to the relevant hub.
  • Infrastructure pages – legal, privacy, methodology and directory pages that describe how the project works.

This hub-and-spoke structure makes it easier to keep educational content separate from product listings and to keep explanations consistent across many domains.

Types of Hubs Inside the Network

Within The CreditCard Collection, hubs are grouped by topic rather than by brand or issuer. Common categories include:

  • Travel & FX hubs – how cards behave abroad, FX fees, travel rewards and protections.
  • Technology & payments hubs – card rails, NFC, wallets, virtual cards and authentication.
  • Loyalty & rewards hubs – airline, hotel and bank-point ecosystems.
  • Credit score & rebuild hubs – how card usage interacts with files and scoring models.
  • Business & freelance hubs – cards used for company, freelance or side-hustle expenses.

Hub pages are meant to be starting points. They summarise the landscape and then link down to narrower guides and minisites for more detailed reading.

Spokes, Redirects and Documentation Pages

Not every .creditcard domain is a full article. Some domains host minisites, others act as simple redirects into the closest relevant hub or documentation page.

In all cases, the goal is clarity: users should quickly see whether they are on an explainer page, a comparison page or a structural page (privacy, terms, disclaimers). Marketing-style “best card” claims are intentionally avoided.

Neutrality, Disclosures and Independence

The project is operated by ronarn AS and is designed to be transparent about roles and relationships. When commercial links are present, they are clearly marked and do not change the core educational content.

Hub.Creditcard itself does not recommend individual cards. It describes how the ecosystem is organized so that readers can navigate to the right hubs and documentation and interpret product information more confidently.
